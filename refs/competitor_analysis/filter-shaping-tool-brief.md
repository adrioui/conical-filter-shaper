# Product/Mechanism Brief: Universal Conical Filter Shaping Tool
*Competitive landscape, design patterns, failure modes & whitespace — April 2025*

---

## 1. Products Mapped

### OREA Neo Negotiator + Tulip (2024–2026)
- **Segment:** Flat-bottom / OREA-ecosystem
- **Price:** $22–$38 USD (Negotiator alone); Tulip ~€16 (3D-printed ECO-PLA, London)
- **Mechanism:** Two-piece. *Tulip* is a pre-folding jig — lay flat filter on top, press Neo Negotiator down → 4-flap shape created in ~5 sec, fold flaps over, pull out together. Then insert shaped filter + Negotiator into brewer, pre-wet through tool's flow holes, rotate 90° and remove. Paper hugs walls with zero-bypass contact.
- **Material:** Neo Negotiator = injection-molded BPA-free PET, heat-safe to boiling; Tulip = ECO-PLA (3D-printed)
- **Compatibility:** V2, V3/MK2, V4 *Narrow*, O1, Glass, Porcelain Brewer — **NOT V4 Wide** (requires separate Wide SKU). Flat-bottom only; no conical support.
- **Origin:** Originated as a free STL on Printables 4 yrs ago; Neo Negotiator is the first injection-molded iteration.

### UFO Folding Template + Disputer
- **Segment:** Wide-angle (80°) conical — UFO Dripper
- **Price:** Template included free in UFO box; "Disputer" press tool sold separately (~$20+)
- **Mechanism:** Flat 2D template/diagram (or small white plastic guide) teaches three fold methods to convert standard 60° cone filters (V60-01/02) into 80° angle required by UFO's unusual geometry. Three community-validated methods: (1) fold-and-tear seam, (2) double-fold flip, (3) symmetric double-fold. Compatible with Sibarist flat papers directly; template needed only for standard conical papers.
- **Material:** Bundled plastic template (polypropylene-style); Disputer = separate press tool
- **Compatibility:** UFO Dripper only. Converts any 02-size cone/flat filter.
- **Key differentiator:** Bundled inclusion signals "tool is table stakes for non-native filter use."

### Suprima SD1R Ruler
- **Segment:** Steep-angle (48°) conical — SD1R semi-immersion dripper
- **Price:** Bundled with SD1R dripper (~$50–70 retail). Ruler not sold separately.
- **Mechanism:** SS304 stainless ruler acts as a fold-guide/scoring jig to fold standard 01-size conical papers (Hario V60-01, Origami 2-cup) to the SD1R's 48° geometry. Precise fold measurement ensures consistent drape angle. Ruler doubles as a compact holder/stand (tiny form factor, travel-friendly).
- **Material:** SS304 ruler + polycarbonate dripper body (heat resistant to 120°C)
- **Compatibility:** SD1R 48° angle only. Standard V60-01 size filters.
- **Key differentiator:** Metal (durable, dishwasher-adjacent), bundled, serves secondary role as a stand.

### Brew Methods Filter Paper Applicator – Conical
- **Segment:** Multi-brewer conical (hold-only, no folding)
- **Price:** ~$22 USD (AUD pricing)
- **Mechanism:** Simplest of all — place pre-folded paper in brewer, lower applicator inside to hold it flush against walls, pre-wet through it, remove applicator. Holds but does not fold/shape. 6.8 cm H × 7.9 cm W.
- **Material:** Food-grade Tritan, transparent, heat-resistant to 109°C
- **Compatibility:** V60, KONO, Origami, Cafec Flower, Kinto + others. Both 1-cup and 2-cup sizes.
- **Key differentiator:** Explicit multi-brewer support in a single conical SKU, lowest workflow complexity.

### Prism Paper Filter Negotiator (Fresh-Delight/Kohiraifu)
- **Segment:** Multi-brewer (conical + flat-bottom, two SKUs)
- **Price:** ~$22.40 USD
- **Mechanism:** Holds filter flush against dripper walls during pre-wetting. 20-rib internal structure aligns with pleated filter seams, reducing filter collapse and micro-gaps. No pre-fold jig included — filter is pre-folded by hand, then Prism holds it during wet-down.
- **Material:** Eastman Tritan™, BPA-free, food-grade, up to 105°C
- **Conical SKU:** Max Ø 7.8 cm / Min Ø 3.5 cm → V60, April, Cafec Flower, Origami, Kōno, Kalita 185
- **Flat-Bottom SKU:** Max Ø 6.6 cm / Min Ø 4.2 cm → OREA, Kalita 155
- **Key differentiator:** Explicit published compat list, ribbed interior for pleated filters, genuine multi-brand conical support.

### Fellow Stagg X Smoosher / Negotiator
- **Segment:** Flat-bottom / Stagg X specific
- **Price:** $16–$19 USD (community/Etsy resellers; not an official Fellow product)
- **Mechanism:** 3D-printed press tool to fold and seat flat-bottom Stagg X filters for no-bypass extraction. Direct geometry copy of Stagg X interior.
- **Material:** PLA (food-safety and heat-resistance concerns at boiling temps)
- **Compatibility:** Stagg X only
- **Key differentiator:** Community-origin, proves market demand before official products appear.

### Aboiled Adjustable Filter Shaping Tool (Amazon OEM)
- **Segment:** Universal/adjustable conical claim
- **Price:** $33–$34 USD
- **Mechanism:** Mechanical adjustment spanning 36°–60° to accommodate different conical brewer angles. Purpose-built for multi-brewer use.
- **Material:** Unknown (appears injection-molded plastic, limited spec disclosure)
- **Compatibility:** Claims 36°–60° range (covers V60 60°, but misses UFO 80° and SD1R 48°)
- **Key issue:** Low community adoption, minimal reviews, perceived as generic OEM. Doesn't reach steeper or wider angles.

## 2. Core Design Patterns

- **Geometry-matching is the only mechanism that matters.** Every tool is fundamentally a replica of a brewer's interior taper angle and diameter. Accuracy of angle is the product's only real claim to performance.

- **Two-stage workflow dominates:** (1) *pre-fold jig* shapes paper off-brewer, (2) *insertion/retention tool* holds it during wet-down. OREA Tulip+Negotiator is the clearest two-piece example. Products that collapse both stages into one (Prism, Brew Methods) are simpler but sacrifice pre-fold precision.

- **Water-activation is universal.** All tools rely on wetting the filter *while the tool is still in place* to "set" paper against the wall. No product uses mechanical retention (clip, collar, pin) to hold dry; all assume pre-wet as the activation step.

- **Material evolution is clear:** Community 3D-printed PLA → commercial Tritan/PET. Heat-resistance and food-safety are now table-stakes for injection-molded products. PLA tools still persist on Etsy but are second-tier.

- **Ribs and flow channels are becoming standard:** OREA Neo Negotiator uses radial holes for water flow-through. Prism uses 20 internal ribs to align with pleated seams. Both address the hidden failure mode of filter seam bunching.

- **Bundling strategy is emerging:** SD1R and UFO bundle their tools. This raises expectations that any new dripper ecosystem *should* include a shaping accessory.

## 3. User Workflow (Consensus Steps)

```
1. Pull flat/cone filter from sleeve
2. [If needed] Pre-fold using jig or fold-guide to approximate brewer angle
3. Hold pre-folded filter → press tool into it OR place filter in brewer + lower tool
4. Check centering (mis-centering is the #1 user error)
5. Pre-wet through or around tool (water activates paper to hold shape)
6. Gently rotate tool if applicable, then remove
7. Inspect wall contact — no visible air gaps
8. Add ground coffee, begin brew
```
Fastest workflow (OREA Tulip + Neo): ~5–10 sec total setup.
Slowest: multi-fold manual methods (UFO hand-fold without template).

## 5. Likely Failure Modes

- **Mis-centering before press:** Filter off-center on jig → one flap longer → bypass gap on short side. (OREA Tulip explicitly warns about this.) No current product has a self-centering guide.

- **Fold spring-back:** Pre-folded filter re-opens before or during tool insertion. Affects stiffer papers (Abaca, thick Japanese-milled). Worsens when operator delays pre-wetting.

- **Seam bunching / pleat misalignment:** Pleated (ridged) cone filters (e.g., Abaca T-90, Cafec tabbed) have seams that don't compress flush. Tools without rib-guides leave micro-gaps at seams. Prism's 20-rib design is the only product explicitly addressing this.

- **Heat degradation of PLA tools:** Community 3D-printed tools warp, delaminate, or leach with repeated boiling-water exposure. Heat-safe Tritan products avoid this but cost 2–4× more.

- **SKU mismatch:** Buying Narrow negotiator for a Wide brewer, or using 60° tool with a 48° or 80° dripper. Community threads show significant confusion around the OREA Narrow vs. Wide split. No product has a "fits all" solution.

- **Insufficient pre-wet area:** If water only reaches the top half of the filter during pre-wetting (common with faster pours), the bottom half collapses after tool removal.

- **Paper puncture at fold seams:** Hard-pressed folds on thin filters (Sibarist FAST, Timemore 0.05mm thin papers) can develop micro-tears at fold intersections. No tool has a soft-contact tip.

- **Conical angle range gap:** No commercial tool reaches the full 48°–80° spectrum. Aboiled stops at 60°. UFO template solves 80° only for UFO. SD1R ruler solves 48° only for SD1R.

## 6. Whitespace for a Universal Conical Filter Shaping Product

### 🔴 High-priority gaps (clear demand, no good solution)

- **Adjustable conical angle with preset stops:** The 36°–60° Aboiled exists but is clunky/poorly received. A Tritan-injection tool with *click-stops* at 48° (SD1R), 60° (V60/Origami/April), and 80° (UFO) would address 95% of the conical market in one SKU. Mechanism analogy: adjustable tamper with depth stops.

- **Conical pre-fold + seat in one tool (vs. hold-only):** Every current conical tool (Prism, Brew Methods) only *holds* a pre-folded filter. No product does for conicals what the OREA Tulip+Negotiator does for flat-bottoms: *creates* the fold geometry off-brewer and then seats it. A conical with a collapsible pre-fold base (like a expanding cone mould) could fill this gap.

- **Self-centering filter seat:** All current tools rely on operator accuracy for centering. A tool with a radial tab, registration ring, or optical mark on the jig/base that aligns the filter's seam could eliminate the #1 failure mode.

### 🟡 Medium-priority gaps (real but smaller market)

- **Cup-size switchable (1-cup / 2-cup):** Most tools are size-specific. A flip-reversible or telescoping tool handling both sizes (01/02 filter standards) in one piece removes a common purchase decision barrier.

- **Mechanical dry-hold (no pre-wet dependency):** An optional locking collar or soft spring grip that holds the filter mechanically (not relying on water activation) enables pre-staged setup — filter loaded dry, then brewer carried to kettle rather than pouring in place. Useful for competition staging, cafes.

- **Rib/guide for pleated vs. unpleated filter modes:** A reconfigurable rib pattern (e.g., collapsible ribs or a rib/no-rib sleeve) could handle both pleated conical papers *and* smooth flat papers adapted to conical geometry.

### 🟢 Lower-priority / positioning gaps

- **Premium material at sub-$25 for the adjustable category:** The only adjustable option is $33+ and OEM-generic. A Tritan/PC injection-molded adjustable product at $18–22 would win on both value and quality perception vs. the Aboiled.

- **Travel form factor:** SD1R ruler hints at the demand. A compact tool (filter shaper + drip stand function + clip-carry) for the on-the-go brewer is underserved, particularly for backpacking/camp coffee contexts.

- **Brewer-agnostic ecosystem identity:** All current tools brand themselves around one dripper. A tool that *leads with cross-compatibility* (published compat chart, angle-mark labeling) and commits to multi-brand support as its core identity would earn community trust and SEO differentiation.
